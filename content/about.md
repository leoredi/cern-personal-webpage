Federico Leo Redi is a tenure-track Assistant Professor of Physics at the University of Bergamo and INFN Milan, working on searches for new physics with the LHCb experiment.

- Research focus: long-lived particles, electroweak studies, analysis preservation, and data processing leadership at LHCb.
- Senior Research Fellow, CERN EP Department (2021–2023).
- Postdoctoral Researcher, EPFL High Energy Physics Laboratory (2017–2021).
- PhD in Fundamental Physics, Imperial College London (2017); BSc and MSc in Physics with Distinction, Imperial College London (2013).
- Leadership: Convenor for LHCb QCD, Electroweak, and Exotica Physics Group (2022–2024); co-convenor for LHC Long-Lived Particles WG (2019–2022) and LHCb Exotica and Higgs Sub-group (2019–2021).
- Service: LHCb Editorial Board Member (since 2021), institutional reviewer since 2015, journal referee for Physics Letters B, EPJ C, and SciPost Physics Core.
